About

Latinx Parents for Change is a project designed to build on leadership skills, deepen understanding of school governance and promote the active participation of Toronto’s Latinx families in school and community life.

If you would like to learn more about our project, please contact Alexandra Arraiz at:

Phone: (647) 894-0239

Email: aarraiz@workingwomencc.org

You can also visit the Working Women website.

Working Women Community Centre and The Mennonite New Life Centre offer FREE training for Latinx parents to become active advocates for their children’s education. During the program, parents learn how the Ontario Education System works, and how to get involved in school councils and/or parental involvement committees to initiate change in their children’s schools. Parents are required to commit to the full 27-hour training. Childcare and tokens are provided for participants.

History

Working Women is a charitable organization that provides a variety of programs and services, which help immigrant women adjust to living in Toronto. When asked what sets us apart from other charities in the sector, we responded with: "Working Women is unique in two very distinct ways — our enterprising spirit, and our approach with clients."

We see possibilities and take action.

Latinx Parents for Change was created as part of the Community Legacy Fund established after the PanAm/Parapan Am Games held in Toronto in 2015.

The Community Legacy Initiative invests in projects that profile and provide longer-term economic and/or social infrastructure benefits for Toronto's Latin American, South American, and Caribbean communities.

Working Women, Mennonite Centre, and LAEN applied to this Legacy Fund in order to run a program which would focus on Latinx parents and caregivers. From WWCC's experience with the On Your Mark program, we knew that parents needed information and tools to better navigate the school system and become more effective advocates for their children. The program was ran in partnership with LAEN and MCNLT for the first year and with MCNLT for the second year.

The short-term goal of the project is to increase Latin American parent engagement in schools, specifically the representation of Latin American parents on School Councils.

We aim to:

  • Increase knowledge, skills and leadership capacity among Latin American parents,
  • Increase engagement of Latin American parents in schools.

The long-term goal of the project is to improve social and economic outcomes for Latin American children, youth, and families in Toronto.

We aim to:

  • Improve education outcomes for Latin American children attending schools where there is increased engagement of Latin American parents.



Historia

Working Women es una organizacion de caridad que provee carios programas y servicios con el fin de ayudar a mujeres immigrantes a ajustarse a la vida en Toronto. Cuando nos preguntan que nos diferencia de otras agencias del sector, respondemos, "Working Women es unica en dos maneras — nuestro espiritu enprendedor y nuestra manera."

Vemos posibilidades y tomamos acción.

Latinx Parents for Change se creó como parte del Community Legacy Fund establecido después de los Juegos Panamericanos / Parapanamericanos celebrados en Toronto en 2015.

La Iniciativa de Legado Comunitario (Community Legacy Initiative) invirtió en proyectos que perfilaran y proporcionaran beneficios de infraestructura económica y / o social a largo plazo para las comunidades de América Latina, América del Sur y el Caribe que residen en Toronto.

Working Women, Mennonite Center y LAEN aplicaron a este Legacy Fund para ejecutar un programa que se enfocaría en los padres y cuidadores Latinx. A partir de la experiencia de WWCC con el programa On Your Mark, sabíamos que los padres necesitaban información y herramientas para navegar mejor el sistema escolar y convertirse en abogados más efectivos de sus hijos. El programa se ejecutó en asociación con LAEN y MCNLT durante el primer año y con MCNLT por segundo año.

El objetivo a corto plazo del proyecto es aumentar la participación de los padres de América Latina en las escuelas, específicamente la representación de los padres de América Latina en los Consejos Escolares.

Apuntamos a:

  • Aumentar el conocimiento, las habilidades y la capacidad de liderazgo entre los padres de América Latina,
  • Aumentar la participación de padres latinoamericanos en las escuelas.

El objetivo a largo plazo del proyecto es mejorar los resultados sociales y económicos para los niños, jóvenes y familias de América Latina en Toronto.

Apuntamos a:

  • Mejorar los resultados educativos para los niños latinoamericanos que asisten a escuelas donde hay una mayor participación de padres latinoamericanos.

Description

The program aims to train 6 cohorts of parents over 3 years. This free program is made up of 8 training sessions (1 per week). During the 8 weeks, we will be focusing on the following topics:

  1. Canadian political system
  2. Education system in Ontario
  3. Structures of parental involvement in schools
  4. Navigating the school system with an equity and social justice lens
  5. Identifying and overcoming challenges in Special Education
  6. Promoting Active Parental Involvement
  7. Leadership building
  8. Public speaking and effective communication

After the 8 weeks, we will work with participants to develop an Action Project. This is a way of applying all the knowledge and skills acquired throughout the program. This Action Project can consist of delivering a workshop to other parents, organizing an event at your child's school, participating in a community event, and more. The program facilitators work with the group to define the action and then follow it through. At the end of this action component, participants will receive a Certificate of Participation and will be ready to take part in the school council, School Board Committees, or any other intervention space within the education system that interests you — and from a more informed and empowered perspective.

Some of our previous Action Projects have inclued:

  • Community Forums
  • Workshop series through community group Reflejo de Mujer
  • Workshops at community spaces like The Neighbourhood Office
  • Workshops at 4 Villages Community Centre
  • Information sessions for family and friends held at participants' homes and at Working Women's Community Room

Descripción

El programa tiene como objetivo capacitar a 6 grupos de padres/madres/cuidadorxs durante 3 años. Este programa gratuito, consiste en 8 sesiones de capacitación (1 por semana). Durante estas 8 semanas estaremos tratando los siguientes temas:

  1. Sistema político de Canadá
  2. Sistema educativo de Ontario
  3. Estructuras de participación de padres en las escuelas
  4. Navegando el sistema escolar con un lente de equidad y justicia social
  5. Identificando y superando las dificultades en la Educación Especial
  6. Promoviendo la participación de los padres
  7. Liderazgo comunitario
  8. Hablar en público, comunicación efectiva

Después de las 8 semanas, trabajaremos con lxs participantes para desarrollar un proyecto de acción. Esto es una manera de poner en práctica los conocimientos y herramientas del programa. Este proyecto puede ser dictar un taller a otros padres/cuidadorxs, organizar un evento en el colegio de sus niñxs, participar en un evento comunitario, etc. Lxs facilitadorxs del programa trabajarían con el grupo y lxs integrantes para definir la acción y llevarla a cabo. Al final de este componente activo, lxs participantes recibirán un certificado de capacitación y estarán listxs para formar parte de ‘school councils’, comités en los school boards, o cualquier otro espacio de intervención en el cual estén interesados. Algunos de nuestros proyectos de acción pasados han incluido:

  • Foros Comunitarios
  • Talleres con el grupo comunitario Reflejo de Mujer
  • Talleres con organizaciones como The Neighbourhood Office
  • Talleres en 4 Villages Community Centre
  • Charlas informativas con padres en diferentes localidades (informales y formales)

Description

PLACE stands for "Latinx Parents Advocacy Committe for Equity in Education". This group was formed from Latinx Project alumni who wanted to continue the advocacy work they had begun as parents in the project.

Our Vision

That our children are educated to the fullest of their natural abilities, based on equity principles.

Our Mission

PLACE is an advocacy group for Latinx parents that promote the excellence in childrens' education within a peer-mentoring environment.

If you are interested in learning more about us and coming to one of our information sessions, subscribe to our updates!
